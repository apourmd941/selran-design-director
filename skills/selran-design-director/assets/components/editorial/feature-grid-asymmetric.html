Chapter One · The Ledger

The case for a correct-as-you-go account of the business.

The close was an artifact

For a century, the monthly close was a consequence of paper and postage — numbers had to be gathered, then reconciled, then written down. The ritual survived the paper.

What {Brand} offers is not a faster close. It's the absence of one: every transaction posts, categorizes, and reconciles at the instant it happens.

A product, not a report

The ledger is the product. Not an export, not a dashboard that summarizes an export — the thing itself. Drill from the P&L line to the receipt in two clicks. Override a rule and leave a trail. Let the auditor read the history.

Trust is the feature

When the numbers are right on Tuesday, they are right on Friday. That is the whole argument. Every feature — cards, treasury, forecasts — exists only because the ledger underneath can be trusted. Nothing else would survive a Series B.
