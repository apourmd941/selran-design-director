The cover story

The internet is fine. The feed is the problem.

By Amara Wole Editor-in-chief Published April 2026

We have tried, issue after issue, to avoid the easy argument. The timeline is bad. The web is dying. The feed is eating itself. These are the slogans of the exhausted. They are, by now, a kind of weather — complained about, endured, still there tomorrow.

But when you read thirty-two essays in a row from writers who stopped scrolling, something else shows up. The internet they describe is not dead. It is patient. It is a place you go on purpose, for a reason, for a while. It is mostly quiet. It rewards the things a feed punishes: length, second thoughts, a thing written once and kept.
