# Pack commerce (v3.4 + Phase 16)

How paid Selran packs and the Enterprise tier are actually bought. This reference is for pack buyers, agencies, and enterprise prospects — the person writing the check, not the pack author. The author-side workflow (how a pack gets *listed* in the catalog) lives in `pack-distribution.md` and `pack-authoring.md`.

## Purpose

The core Selran Design Director skill is MIT and stays MIT. Paid content — the 22 commercial Wave-1 and Wave-2 packs and the two Enterprise SKUs — rides on top of it through a thin commerce layer that uses Stripe Checkout for payment, a signed webhook for key issuance, and a transactional-email pair for delivery. Nothing in the skill itself phones home; the commerce layer is an operator-side service that knows nothing about which pack you have installed once the email has gone out. See `pack-telemetry.md` for what is and isn't recorded — purchase pages run no analytics and the transactional emails carry no tracking pixels, which is deliberate.

This document explains the purchase surface from the customer's point of view. If you want the entitlement model (what a license *grants*), read `pack-enterprise.md` and the `LICENSE` / `LICENSE-ENTERPRISE.md` files at the repo root. If you want the install mechanics (how `selran pack install` honors a license), read `pack-distribution.md`. This doc sits in the middle: from "how do I pay" to "how does the license arrive."

## What you can buy

Three buckets. Pricing is fixed for per-pack SKUs and negotiated for Enterprise.

**MIT seed packs (free, no purchase):**

- `oss-project`, `personal-blog`, `resume-cv`

Install directly via `selran pack install <name>` — no license ack, no email, no money.

**Wave-1 Industry packs ($49 each, perpetual single-org license):**

- `fintech`, `healthcare`, `saas-b2b`, `developer-tools`, `e-commerce`

**Wave-1 Archetype packs ($29 each, perpetual single-org license):**

- `challenger`, `luxury`, `mission-driven`, `tech-forward`, `heritage`, `playful-consumer`, `quiet-professional`

**Wave-2 Industry packs ($49 each):**

- `legal-tech`, `edtech`, `real-estate`, `nonprofit`, `gov-civic`

**Wave-2 Archetype packs ($29 each):**

- `academic`, `activist`, `sophisticated-casual`, `hospitable`, `technical-educator`

**Enterprise SKUs (pricing negotiated per-contract):**

- `enterprise-standard` — multi-org across controlled subsidiaries, 3-business-day SLA, 99.5% uptime commitment
- `enterprise-premium` — Standard plus the agency carve-out for end-client deliverables, private-pack hosting, a 4-business-hour critical-response SLA, 99.9% uptime, and a quarterly design-system review

Enterprise pricing is not listed publicly and never quoted in Stripe Checkout. Contact `enterprise@selran.com` to open a conversation. The negotiation step is by design — an enterprise relationship involves seat count, term length, procurement forms, and sometimes a carve-out scope definition; a self-service checkout can't do those things honestly.

## How a per-pack purchase works

Six steps, start to "installed":

1. **Visit `selran.com/pack/<pack-name>`.** The pack page shows the pack README, screenshots, and a Buy button. No login required; no account to create. Selran does not keep "customer accounts" — your relationship with a pack is the license acknowledgment on your machine, not a user row in a database somewhere.
2. **Click Buy.** You're redirected to a Stripe-hosted Checkout page. Selran never sees your card details — Stripe handles the payment surface end-to-end. The Checkout URL is generated by `commerce/stripe/checkout.py` from the SKU in `commerce/catalog.yaml`; the session metadata carries `sku`, `pack_name`, `license_target`, and `kind` so the webhook can dispatch correctly without having to look anything up.
3. **Complete Checkout.** Enter email and card, confirm. Stripe returns you to a thank-you page on `selran.com`. The thank-you page itself does nothing — it just confirms Stripe accepted the charge. All real work happens server-side in the webhook.
4. **Receive two emails.** The first is the receipt (`purchase-confirmation.html`) and arrives within seconds. The second is the license delivery (`key-delivery.html`, per-pack branch) and arrives within 5 minutes under the SLO. They are deliberately split: the receipt lands even if license issuance is briefly slow, and the receipt carries the order ID you'll need if you have to contact support before the license arrives. The license-delivery email contains a YAML acknowledgment block.
5. **Add the acknowledgment to `.pack-licenses.yaml`.** Paste the block into `.pack-licenses.yaml` in your project root (project-scoped) or `~/.selran/.pack-licenses.yaml` (machine-scoped). Whitespace matters; copy the block as-shown. Project-scoped wins over machine-scoped on conflict, so a team can standardize on project-scoped for CI reproducibility and still let individual developers carry machine-scoped licenses for personal side projects.
6. **Install the pack.** Run `selran pack install <pack-name>`. The CLI reads the acknowledgment, verifies it against the pack's signature, and installs under your entitlement. No activation step, no phone-home. Subsequent `selran pack install --from-lock` invocations in CI honor the same acknowledgment without re-prompting.

The 5-minute delivery window is an SLO, not a guarantee. If it's been 30 minutes and nothing has arrived (check your spam folder first), write to `support@selran.com` with the order ID from the receipt. The issuer logs are keyed on that ID, and support can resend the acknowledgment directly. The most common cause of non-delivery is a corporate email filter rejecting HTML from unfamiliar senders — if you're buying for a company, add `@selran.com` to the allowed-senders list in your email provider before you check out, not after.

## How an enterprise purchase works

Five steps, measured in days, not minutes:

1. **Email `enterprise@selran.com`.** Include: company name, approximate seat count, whether you need Standard or Premium, contract term (1 year is the default; 2 and 3 years are common), and any procurement / legal requirements on your side (W-9, DPA, MSA templates, etc.).
2. **Negotiate scope and price.** A short call or a few email rounds. Price scales with seats, term length, and tier. Agency carve-out (Premium) is priced as a function of expected end-client count; private-pack hosting pricing depends on pack count and bandwidth.
3. **Stripe invoice is issued.** You receive a Stripe Invoice email with a payment link. Net-30 is available on request; most customers pay by ACH or card.
4. **Payment triggers key delivery.** Once Stripe confirms payment, the webhook runs the enterprise license-issuer and sends `key-delivery.html` (enterprise branch) to the billing contact. The key is 80-ish characters, format `selran-ent-<org-slug>-<tier>-<32-char-Crockford-base32>`, signed offline with ed25519.
5. **Register the key.** On every machine that needs Enterprise entitlements, run `selran enterprise register --key <key>`. CI/CD runners can set `SELRAN_ENTERPRISE_LICENSE_KEY` in the environment instead. The CLI verifies the signature on every invocation — no phone-home.

Typical first-contact-to-key is 3-5 business days. Faster is possible if your side is ready (no MSA redlines, no procurement portal); slower happens when legal reviews take their time. There is no self-service enterprise checkout and there will not be one. The conversation is the feature — an enterprise license defines an organizational relationship that includes SLA responsibilities on Selran's side and use-scope commitments on yours, and both of those are worth five minutes on a call to get right rather than dropping through a form.

Once the key is registered, the CLI works fully offline — the signature is verified against the embedded public key (`registry/keys/selran-ent-2026-01.pub`), not a remote API. You can rotate keys yourself by running `selran enterprise register --key <new-key>` with a superseding key from Selran; the previous key is retired without any server-side coordination required. See `pack-enterprise.md` for the operational details.

## What arrives in the key-delivery email

Illustrative only. Never use any of these strings as real credentials.

**Per-pack (YAML acknowledgment block, to paste verbatim into `.pack-licenses.yaml`):**

```yaml
fintech:
  license: commercial-v1
  issued_to: customer@example.invalid
  issued_at: 2026-04-23T14:22:00Z
  order_id: cs_test_AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA
  signature: AAAAAAAA-AAAAAAAA-AAAAAAAA-AAAAAAAA
```

**Enterprise (single-line key string):**

```
selran-ent-org-placeholder-standard-AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA
```

The enterprise key is one line, 80-ish characters, never wrapped. If your terminal wraps it on paste, the CLI will still accept it, but the email template uses `white-space: nowrap` + `overflow-x: auto` on the display box so you can see it intact on mobile (you'll need to hscroll).

## License scope

**Per-pack: Selran Commercial License v1.0.** Single-organization perpetual use. Your organization may install the pack on any machine, in any number of projects, as long as the work product is used within the purchasing organization. Transfer to another organization is not permitted without an explicit transfer agreement (contact `support@selran.com`). If you refund within the 14-day window, the license terminates with the refund. See `LICENSE` at the repo root for the full terms. Per-pack licenses never expire and never renew.

**Enterprise: Selran Enterprise License v1.0.** Annual or multi-year contract. Standard covers multiple controlled subsidiaries — "controlled" meaning majority-owned, same reporting structure, or equivalent — across every seat in the agreed count. Premium adds the agency carve-out, which extends license coverage to deliverables produced *for* third-party end-clients, under the append-only `selran enterprise add-client` audit log. Premium also includes private-pack hosting (your org ships packs to your own CDN behind the `selran enterprise private-registry set <url>` fallback), a 4-business-hour critical-response SLA, 99.9% uptime, and a quarterly design-system review. See `LICENSE-ENTERPRISE.md` for the full terms and `ENTERPRISE-SLA.md` for the severity matrix and service-credit schedule.

## Refunds + cancellation

**Per-pack:** 14-day no-questions-asked refund. Email `support@selran.com` with the order ID; refund is processed by Stripe within 5-10 business days depending on your bank. Refunding the pack does *not* automatically delete the pack's files from your disk — that would be a DMCA-adjacent overreach, and the CLI does not walk your filesystem looking for things to remove. Instead, the honor system applies: the refunded license is invalidated server-side (the signature will no longer verify against a current registry snapshot), and the customer is expected to remove the acknowledgment from `.pack-licenses.yaml`. Continued use after refund is a license violation, not a technical block.

**Enterprise:** refunds are per-contract — there is no blanket 14-day policy. Most contracts include a pro-rated cancellation clause for material SLA breach. Cancellation or refund triggers a revocation: the key is appended to `registry/keys/revocation-list.json`, which every CLI honors within 24 hours (the revocation list is fetched on `selran pack install` and cached). Revoked licenses cannot install new packs and cannot resolve private-registry entries. Packs already installed continue to work under their original per-pack licenses — revocation is scoped to the Enterprise entitlement, not to work already done.

## Renewal cadence

**Per-pack: no renewal.** The license is perpetual. You paid once; you keep it.

**Enterprise: 90-day advance reminder.** The operator sends `renewal-reminder.html` at 90, 60, 30, and 7 days before `EXPIRY_DATE` on every active Enterprise contract. If you renew inside the 30-day-post-expiry grace window, coverage backfills to the original expiry date with no gap. After 30 days post-expiry, coverage restarts on the new signature date. Lapsed coverage pauses the Enterprise entitlement (no new private-pack installs, no new agency carve-out entries, no SLA coverage) but does not revoke any per-pack license. Packs installed during the Enterprise term continue to work under their existing per-pack licenses after Enterprise lapse — this is deliberate: a lapsed relationship should not cause a production outage.

## Renewal cadence in practice

Most enterprise customers renew on an anniversary cycle — the 1-year term expires, they sign a continuation. Some customers use renewal as an opportunity to change tier (Standard → Premium, typically, once agency work starts appearing in the pipeline) or to adjust seat count. The renewal conversation is a chance to do both. If you know you'll need more seats before expiry, you can file a mid-term seat expansion at any time; pricing is pro-rated to the remaining term and the new expiry matches the original.

The 90-day reminder is designed to give procurement-heavy customers enough runway to get a PO through their own system before the current term ends. If your procurement cycle is longer than 90 days — some public-sector customers run on 180-day cycles — email `enterprise@selran.com` and we will start the reminders earlier for your specific contract. There is no automatic escalation; the template only fires at the cadence the operator sets.

If a contract lapses entirely, the Enterprise entitlement pauses but the pack installs you made during the term continue to work under their per-pack licenses. This matters: a missed procurement deadline should never cause a production incident for a design system. The per-pack licenses predate the Enterprise grant and survive its lapse — Enterprise is additive, not a container that takes the packs with it when it goes away.

## Security posture on the purchase surface

The commerce layer is designed to minimize what Selran sees and stores.

- **Stripe handles all card data.** Selran never touches raw PANs; we never see a CVV. Stripe's PCI scope is Stripe's; we are not in it. If you need proof of PCI compliance for your own procurement process, Stripe's AOC is the right document, not anything we could produce.
- **Webhook signature verification is mandatory.** Every inbound `checkout.session.completed` event is verified against `STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET` before the issuer runs. An unsigned or mis-signed event raises `WebhookError` and is not processed.
- **Customer email is the only PII we retain.** We keep it for license delivery, renewal reminders, and support correspondence. We do not sell it, do not share it with analytics vendors, and do not cross-reference it with third-party data brokers.
- **No analytics, no tracking pixels on the purchase or email surface.** The purchase pages run no analytics beyond Stripe's own minimum required for Checkout. The transactional emails carry no open-tracking pixels, no click-tracking redirects, and no third-party domains. This is a hard rule because `pack-telemetry.md` sets the pack-side privacy posture; the commerce surface has to match it or the whole promise collapses.
- **Enterprise keys are stored encrypted at rest** in the license-issuer database, keyed by order ID. Key material is only emitted to the customer once, in `key-delivery.html`. If a customer loses the key, we can reissue from the encrypted record — but reissue is a `security@selran.com` conversation, not a self-service flow, because reissuing a live key is an operation that deserves human review.
- **The webhook archives every event to an append-only JSONL file** (`commerce/orders.jsonl` by default; configurable via `SELRAN_ORDER_ARCHIVE_PATH`). Enterprise license key material is NOT included in the archive — only the key's prefix (first four dash-separated segments) for debugging. This means a compromise of the archive does not leak live keys.
- **Refund and dispute flows require their own signature verification.** A `charge.refunded` or `charge.dispute.created` event that arrives without a valid Stripe signature is treated as a webhook attack attempt, logged, and dropped. Operators should never "manually trigger" a refund flow by crafting an event — use the Stripe dashboard or Stripe API, which will produce a correctly-signed event.

## Who to contact

- `support@selran.com` — per-pack purchase issues, license acknowledgment not arriving, refund requests, install problems where the CLI reports a licensing error
- `enterprise@selran.com` — enterprise sales, renewals, seat changes, tier upgrades, agency carve-out setup, private-registry configuration, SLA credit claims
- `security@selran.com` — suspected fraud on a purchase, compromised license key, anything that needs the security rubric in `SECURITY.md`

For billing disputes, start with `support@selran.com`; for anything legal (DPA, MSA, custom contract terms), the enterprise address is the right first stop even if you're asking about a per-pack purchase. Please don't file a Stripe dispute before emailing us — a dispute costs Selran a $15 fee regardless of outcome, and 99% of the time a plain email solves the underlying issue faster.
